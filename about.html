Engineer Profile

The Engineer
Who Builds Software

I'm a Chemical and Process Engineer who learned to code — not a developer who learned some engineering terminology. That distinction matters more than it sounds when you're building tools for real industrial processes.

My background is in chemical and process engineering, with hands-on experience in activated carbon production, comminution and milling operations, rotary kiln processes, and facility management systems. The software I build is grounded in that context — I know what the numbers mean because I've worked with the processes that generate them.

What drew me to building software wasn't a career pivot — it was frustration. The tools available for process engineers are either expensive, generic, or both. So I started building my own. The roller mill PSD optimisation platform started as a personal tool. The stock counting app was built because the plant needed something the Zebra TC26 could actually run. The janitorial management system replaced a clipboard and a spreadsheet.

I now work as a freelancer, taking on projects where engineering domain knowledge and software capability are both genuinely required. If you just need a web app built, there are faster and cheaper options. If you need someone who can read your PSD data, understand why your Rosin-Rammler fit is off, and then build the tool to fix it — that's the gap I fill.
